Who We Are

Who and Where Are We?

Our family has been involved in dog breeding for many years. Located in the quiet town of Russell, MA, just off Route 20, we raise puppies—but we're not a pet store and don't keep regular business hours or always have puppies available. Feel free to call or text us to find out about our current and upcoming litters. Because demand is high, our puppies are often adopted very quickly.

Visiting

When Can You Visit?

Visits are welcome by appointment most days from Monday to Saturday; we're closed on Sundays. Our modern whelping area stays clean and healthy, so please let us know if you've had contact with sick animals before your visit—we might ask you to wear booties, sanitize your hands, or occasionally meet outside, depending on the circumstances. We appreciate your cooperation!

Health & Care

About the Puppy Parents

We prioritize our parent dogs' health with routine veterinary visits, up-to-date vaccinations, and DNA tests to check for genetic issues. This careful monitoring helps ensure we provide healthy puppies and confidently offer a health guarantee. (See our health guarantee)

Our Puppies

About the Puppies

We are dedicated to raising happy, healthy, and beautiful puppies! Every puppy comes to you with a vet-issued health certificate, complete and current vaccination records, and a two-year genetic health guarantee. All of our puppies are also microchipped.

Available Sizes

What Sizes Do We Offer?

Goldendoodles — Micro, Mini, and Mid size

Bernedoodles — Mini and Mid size

Samoyed — Standard size

Micro (16-20 lbs)
Great for families, children, seniors, small homes, apartments, and modest yards.
Mini (20-30 lbs)
Remain manageable for many seniors and are excellent family companions.
Mid Size (30+ lbs)
Well-suited to larger spaces and active lifestyles—perfect for athletic owners and families.
Standard (40+ lbs)
Refers to the full adult size of the mother for any listed breed.

Every size offers its own advantages, allowing you to choose what suits your needs best.

How It Works

About the Process of Adoption

Our adoption process is not complicated... We do focus first and foremost on the New England states and local so rather than having an application process we choose to meet personally with you at some point of the adoption. We are happy to use Zoom on the occasion that distance is a problem, however we still hope to meet you when you pick up your puppy or when we bring the puppy to you.

We do not ship puppies, we expect you will pick them up or we will bring you for an additional fee...

Keeping the transaction personal is a priority and maintaining the friendship beyond the transaction is important to us.
